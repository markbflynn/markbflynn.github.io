 <p>I’m a former Navy officer turned higher education professional. I grew up in Virginia and studied history at William & Mary before earning an MBA from Lynchburg College. During my time in the Navy, I got to drive warships, work with incredible people from all over the country, and learn a lot about how the military operates across air, sea, and undersea mission areas.</p>

<p>After the Navy, I spent a year consulting on anti-submarine warfare systems, then headed back to school for a master’s in Higher Education Administration. I work with computer science students at Virginia Tech as an academic advisor, helping them navigate college and plan for what’s next. I’m especially interested in veterans in higher education, the evolving relationship between education policy and government, and why the gender gap in education attainment keeps growing.</p>


<p>In my free time, I enjoy photography, learning to code, football (the Jacksonville Jaguars), and the Washington Capitals.</p>
